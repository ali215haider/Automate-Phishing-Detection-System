Email Phishing Detection

Safe Email Indicators
  • Personal Greeting
    Uses your actual name, not generic terms
  • Legitimate Sender
    From a verified, expected email address
  • Professional Language
    Proper grammar, spelling, and formatting
  • Expected Communication
    Related to services you actually use
Phishing Red Flags
  • Urgent Action Required
    "Act now," "expires today," "immediate action"
  • Generic Greetings
    "Dear Customer," "Dear User," "Hello there"
  • Suspicious Attachments
    Unexpected .exe, .zip, or document files
  • Requests for Sensitive Info
    Passwords, SSN, credit card details

URL Security Guidelines

Safe URL Characteristics
  • HTTPS Protocol - Look for the lock icon
  • Correct Domain - Matches the expected website
  • Proper Spelling - No typos in the domain name
  • Reasonable Length - Not excessively long
Suspicious Indicators
  • URL Shorteners - bit.ly, tinyurl.com, etc.
  • Too Many Subdomains - Multiple dots in domain
  • Suspicious Characters - @ symbols, hyphens
  • New Domains - Recently registered
Danger Signs
  • IP Addresses - Numbers instead of domain names
  • No HTTPS - Only HTTP, especially for login pages
  • Typosquatting - Similar to popular sites
  • Suspicious TLDs - .tk, .ml, .ga domains

Security Best Practices

Prevention Strategies
Use Strong Passwords

Create unique, complex passwords for each account and use a password manager.

Enable Two-Factor Authentication

Add an extra layer of security with 2FA whenever possible.

Keep Software Updated

Regularly update your browser, OS, and security software.

Verification Steps
Contact Directly

When in doubt, contact the organization through official channels.

Verify URLs

Always check the URL before entering sensitive information.

Take Your Time

Don't rush when dealing with urgent-sounding requests.

Common Phishing Examples

Example Subject: "URGENT: Your account will be suspended"
Red Flags:
  • Generic greeting ("Dear Customer")
  • Urgent language and threats
  • Requests to click links to "verify" account
  • Poor grammar and spelling errors
  • Sender domain doesn't match the bank
What to do: Contact your bank directly through their official website or phone number.

Example: "Your computer has been infected! Call this number immediately!"
Red Flags:
  • Unsolicited calls or pop-ups about computer problems
  • Requests for remote access to your computer
  • Demands for immediate payment
  • Claims to be from Microsoft, Apple, or other tech companies
What to do: Hang up or close the pop-up. Legitimate tech companies don't make unsolicited calls.

Example: "Someone posted a video of you! Click here to see it"
Red Flags:
  • Messages about embarrassing content
  • Fake notifications about tagged photos or videos
  • Links to sites that look like social media login pages
  • Messages from friends' compromised accounts
What to do: Verify with your friend through another communication method before clicking any links.
